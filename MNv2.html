004 · The Cloth

A specification,
in detail.

Featured fabric of the season, from the Brunelli mill outside Biella, woven in lots of fewer than two hundred metres.

Melton
— Brunelli 04·N
Magnified · 28×
680gsm
90% Wool
10% Cashmere
Boiled finish

A blend of merino wool sourced from the Vermandois region of northern France and grade-A cashmere from Inner Mongolia, sorted by hand at our partner mill in Biella.

Both fibres are spun to a 2/30Nm count, plied, and woven on Vandewiele looms dating to the 1970s — slow, careful, and impossible to rush.

  • Wool originVermandois, FR
  • Cashmere originInner Mongolia
  • SpinnerFilatura di Pollone
  • MillBrunelli, Biella

Boiled and milled over forty-four hours to achieve a tight, dense hand. The cloth is wind-resistant without lining, weather-tolerant without coating, and warmer than its weight suggests.

  • Weight680 gsm
  • Width148 cm
  • Weave2/2 twill, milled
  • FinishBoiled, brushed, raised

The cloth softens with wear and improves with age — the brushed surface developing a patina of gentle felting around the cuffs and shoulders within the first season. Air after wearing; brush in the direction of the nap; dry-clean only when necessary, and never more than once a season.

Damage to the cloth is repairable indefinitely through our complimentary atelier service. Send the garment, and we return it.

Every metre carries a traceable lot number recorded against fibre source, dye batch, and weave date. Our supply chain is audited annually by an independent consultancy specialising in slow-fashion provenance.

  • StandardRWS · GCS
  • AuditSlowmark, Paris
  • Lot traceabilityFull chain
